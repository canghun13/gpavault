2025–2026 federal loan interest rates

Federal loan interest rates are set annually by Congress and are fixed for the life of each loan. For loans disbursed July 2025 through June 2026:

Direct Subsidized Loans (undergraduate): 6.39%. Direct Unsubsidized Loans (undergraduate): 6.39%. Direct Unsubsidized Loans (graduate/professional): 7.94%. Direct PLUS Loans (parents and graduate students): 8.94%.

These rates apply to all new loans in the 2025–26 award year. Loans from prior years carry whatever rate was set when they were originally disbursed.

Standard vs extended repayment: what the numbers actually mean

The standard 10-year plan sets up equal monthly payments that pay off your loan fully by year 10. It minimizes total interest paid because you eliminate the principal faster. Extended plans (20 or 25 years) reduce your monthly payment but significantly increase total interest cost.

For a $30,000 loan at 6.39%: the standard 10-year payment is about $334/month and total cost is roughly $40,100. On a 20-year extended plan, the payment drops to $224/month — but total cost rises to about $53,700. That is $13,600 extra in interest for a lower monthly payment.

Grace periods and capitalized interest

Most federal loans have a 6-month grace period after graduation before repayment begins. During a grace period for unsubsidized loans, interest still accrues. At the end of the grace period, any unpaid interest capitalizes — it is added to your principal balance, meaning you will pay interest on that interest for the rest of the loan term.

On a $30,000 unsubsidized loan, 6 months of grace period adds about $960 to your principal before you make a single payment. Consider making interest-only payments during your grace period to prevent this capitalization.
