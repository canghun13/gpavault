Score needed on final exam

The formula this calculator uses

Required Final Score = (Target Grade − Current Grade × (1 − Final Weight)) ÷ Final Weight

For example: current grade 78%, final exam worth 30% of your grade, target 80%. You need (80 − 78 × 0.70) ÷ 0.30 = (80 − 54.6) ÷ 0.30 = 84.7% on the final.

What if the result is over 100%?

If the calculator shows you need more than 100% on the final, your current grade is too low to hit your target through the final alone — regardless of how well you do. At that point, your options are to check whether extra credit is available, talk to your professor about your situation, or adjust your target grade to something achievable. The calculator shows what your maximum possible course grade would be with a perfect final score in that case.

What if the result is negative?

A negative required score means you have already locked in your target grade — even scoring zero on the final would keep you above your goal. This happens when your current grade is so strong that the final cannot bring you below your target. In that case, the final is low stakes and you just need to show up.

Final exam weight varies widely

Final exams typically account for 20% to 40% of a course grade, though some courses have no final at all and others make the final worth 50% or more. Check your syllabus carefully — the weight entered here directly affects the required score calculation. A final worth 20% gives you much less opportunity to move the needle than one worth 40%.
