How this calculator works

Your cumulative GPA is calculated as total quality points divided by total credit hours. To raise your GPA, future A's (4.0) add quality points faster than lower grades do.

Formula: Target GPA × (current credits + future credits) = total quality points needed. Subtract current quality points to find future points needed, then divide by 4.0 (an A) to find the minimum credits.

Can you raise your GPA in one semester?

It depends on how many credits you've already completed. The more credits you have, the harder it is to move the needle. If you have 30 credits at a 3.0 and need a 3.5, you'll need roughly 30 more credits of all A's — that's two to three semesters of perfect grades.

Earlier in your college career, each semester has more impact. If you're in your first year with 15–30 credits, a strong semester can raise your GPA significantly. Use this calculator to see your exact situation.

What counts as an "A"?

This calculator assumes A = 4.0. If you earn A− grades (3.7), your actual results will be slightly lower. For the best-case estimate, 4.0 is the right number to use.
