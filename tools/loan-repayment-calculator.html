Interest saved with extra payments

Why extra payments make such a big difference

Student loan interest accrues daily on your remaining principal. Every extra dollar you pay reduces that principal balance, which means less interest accumulates the following day — and every day after that. Small additional payments compound into large savings over a 10-year repayment window.

For example, on a $35,000 loan at 6.39%, an extra $100 per month saves roughly $2,800 in interest and pays the loan off about 2 years early. An extra $200 per month saves over $4,500 and cuts nearly 4 years off the term.

When to consider extra payments

The best time to make extra payments is early in your loan term, when your principal is highest and interest is accruing fastest. If you receive a tax refund, bonus, or gift, applying it to your loan principal (not just the next payment) has a larger long-term effect than making that same payment later.

Before making extra payments, check whether your servicer applies them to principal or to future payments. You typically need to specify that extra payments should go to principal reduction.

Standard vs extended repayment

Extending your repayment term lowers your monthly payment but significantly increases total interest paid. A $35,000 loan at 6.39% on a standard 10-year plan costs about $47,000 total. On a 25-year extended plan, the same loan costs about $67,000 — an extra $20,000 in interest alone.
