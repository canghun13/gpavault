Your Semester GPA (4.0 scale)

Semester GPA vs. cumulative GPA

Your semester GPA reflects only the courses you took in a single term. Your cumulative GPA is a weighted average of every course across every semester. They're calculated the same way — but semester GPA only uses this semester's data.

Knowing your semester GPA matters when you're checking whether you hit a scholarship requirement for the term, monitoring academic probation thresholds, or seeing how a strong or weak semester is likely to move your cumulative GPA.

How semester GPA is calculated

Formula: Semester GPA = Σ (Grade Points × Credit Hours) ÷ Total Credit Hours this semester

Grade points follow the standard 4.0 scale: A = 4.0, A− = 3.7, B+ = 3.3, B = 3.0, B− = 2.7, C+ = 2.3, C = 2.0, C− = 1.7, D+ = 1.3, D = 1.0, F = 0.0.

Why semester GPA differs from cumulative GPA

A strong semester raises your cumulative GPA — but how much depends on how many credits you've already completed. If you have 90 credits and earn a 4.0 this semester (15 credits), your cumulative GPA only rises by about 0.15 points. If you're a freshman with 15 credits completed, that same semester moves your cumulative GPA dramatically.

Common uses for semester GPA

Students check semester GPA to verify scholarship eligibility (many scholarships require a minimum GPA each term, not just cumulatively), to track progress after academic probation, or simply to see how a tough or easy semester compares to their overall average.
